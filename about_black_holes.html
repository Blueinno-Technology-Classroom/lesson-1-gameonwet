Black holes

Black holes are enigmatic cosmic entities that defy our understanding of space and time. These mysterious gravitational behemoths possess such immense force that not even light can escape their grasp. Exploring the depths of black holes unveils a world where the laws of physics as we know them cease to apply, inviting us to ponder the unfathomable mysteries of the universe.

How they are formed:

    Stellar Formation: A massive star is born from the gravitational collapse of a cloud of gas and dust.
    Main Sequence Life: Throughout its main sequence life, nuclear fusion in the star's core generates energy, balancing gravitational collapse.
    Core Collapse: Gravity overcomes pressure, causing the core to rapidly collapse inwards.
    Supernova Event The collapsing core rebounds, triggering a cataclysmic explosion known as a supernova.

    Neutron Star or Black Hole Formation: Depending on the mass of the core, the remnant may become a neutron star or collapse further into a black hole.
